# Negligence

Negligence is the most common tort claim. It holds a person liable for harm caused by their failure to exercise reasonable care.

## Elements

A plaintiff must prove all four elements to establish negligence:

1. **Duty of care** — the defendant owed a legal duty to the plaintiff (see [[depends-on::duty-of-care]])
2. **Breach** — the defendant's conduct fell below the required standard (see [[depends-on::breach]])
3. **Causation** — the breach actually and proximately caused the harm (see [[depends-on::causation]])
4. **Damages** — the plaintiff suffered actual, compensable harm

If any element fails, the negligence claim fails.

## Standard of Care

The baseline is the **reasonable person standard**: what would a reasonably prudent person have done in the same circumstances?

Special standards apply in certain contexts:

- **Professionals** (doctors, lawyers) are held to the standard of a competent practitioner in their field
- **Children** are held to the standard of a reasonable child of similar age, intelligence, and experience
- **Common carriers** (airlines, buses) owe a heightened duty of care to passengers

## Defenses

- **Contributory negligence** — the plaintiff's own negligence contributed to the harm (complete bar in some jurisdictions)
- **Comparative negligence** — damages reduced by the plaintiff's percentage of fault (most modern jurisdictions)
- **Assumption of risk** — the plaintiff knowingly and voluntarily accepted the risk
- **Statute of limitations** — the claim was filed too late
