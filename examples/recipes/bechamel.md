# Bechamel Sauce

Bechamel is one of the five French mother sauces. It is a white sauce made by adding milk to a white [[depends-on::roux]].

## Ingredients

For approximately 500ml of sauce:

- 45g butter
- 45g all-purpose flour
- 500ml whole milk (warm)
- Salt and white pepper to taste
- Pinch of nutmeg (optional but traditional)

## Method

1. Make a white roux: melt butter, whisk in flour, cook 2-3 minutes without coloring
2. Remove from heat briefly
3. Add warm milk in a slow, steady stream, whisking constantly
4. Return to medium heat
5. Continue whisking until the sauce thickens and comes to a gentle simmer (5-7 minutes)
6. Season with salt, white pepper, and nutmeg
7. Cook 2-3 more minutes to eliminate any raw flour taste

### Tips

- **Warm the milk** — cold milk into hot roux causes lumps. Warm milk incorporates smoothly.
- **Add gradually** — pour a small amount first, whisk until smooth, then add more. Once half the milk is in and smooth, you can add the rest more quickly.
- **White pepper** — used instead of black pepper to keep the sauce white. Black pepper works fine if you don't mind specks.

## Variations

Bechamel is a starting point for many sauces:

| Variation | What to add | Used for |
|-----------|------------|----------|
| **Mornay** | Gruyere and Parmesan cheese | Gratins, mac and cheese, croque monsieur |
| **Soubise** | Sweated onions | Vegetable dishes, eggs |
| **Nantua** | Crayfish butter and cream | Seafood dishes |
| **Mustard sauce** | Dijon mustard | Pork, ham, vegetables |
| **Cream sauce** | Heavy cream | Pasta, chicken, fish |

A basic bechamel keeps in the fridge for 3-4 days. Press plastic wrap directly onto the surface to prevent a skin from forming.
