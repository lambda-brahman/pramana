# Mac and Cheese

A comfort food classic: pasta in a rich cheese sauce. The sauce is a Mornay — a [[depends-on::bechamel]] enriched with cheese, built on a [[depends-on::roux]] foundation.

## Ingredients

Serves 4:

- 400g elbow macaroni (or cavatappi, shells, or penne)
- 45g butter
- 45g flour
- 600ml whole milk (warm)
- 200g sharp cheddar, grated
- 50g Gruyere or Parmesan, grated
- 1 tsp Dijon mustard
- Salt, white pepper, pinch of nutmeg
- Optional topping: 50g breadcrumbs mixed with 20g melted butter

## Method

1. Cook pasta until just al dente (1-2 minutes less than package directions). Drain and set aside.
2. Make a bechamel: melt butter, whisk in flour for 2-3 minutes (white roux), gradually add warm milk, cook until thickened (5-7 minutes).
3. Remove from heat. Stir in the cheese in handfuls, letting each addition melt before adding the next.
4. Add mustard, salt, pepper, and nutmeg.
5. Fold in the cooked pasta.
6. Transfer to a baking dish. Top with breadcrumbs if using.
7. Bake at 190C / 375F for 20-25 minutes until golden and bubbling.

### Tips

- **Undercook the pasta** — it finishes cooking in the oven. Fully cooked pasta turns mushy.
- **Low heat for cheese** — adding cheese to boiling sauce makes it grainy. Remove from heat first.
- **Sharp cheddar** — mild cheddar melts well but tastes flat. Sharp or extra-sharp gives better flavor.
- **Mix cheeses** — cheddar for flavor, Gruyere for stretch, Parmesan for depth.

## Variations

- **Smoky:** Add smoked Gouda or a pinch of smoked paprika
- **Truffle:** Stir in truffle oil just before serving
- **Lobster mac:** Fold in chunks of cooked lobster tail
- **Buffalo chicken:** Add shredded chicken tossed in hot sauce
- **Stovetop only:** Skip the baking step for a creamier, faster version. Just serve directly from the pot.
