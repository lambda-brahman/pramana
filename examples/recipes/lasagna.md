# Lasagna

A layered Italian dish of pasta sheets, meat sauce (ragu), and [[depends-on::bechamel]]. The bechamel provides richness and binds the layers together.

## Layers

A classic lasagna has this structure, repeated 3-4 times:

1. **Ragu** (meat sauce) — spread a thin layer on the bottom
2. **Pasta sheets** — a single layer, no overlapping
3. **Bechamel** — spooned over the pasta
4. **Parmesan** — grated over the bechamel

The top layer should be bechamel and a generous amount of Parmesan for a golden crust.

### Components

**Ragu (meat sauce):**
- 500g ground beef (or a mix of beef and pork)
- 1 onion, 1 carrot, 1 celery stalk — finely diced (soffritto)
- 400g crushed tomatoes
- 150ml red wine
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- Olive oil, salt, pepper

Cook the soffritto until soft, brown the meat, deglaze with wine, add tomatoes. Simmer at least 1 hour (2+ hours is better).

**Bechamel:** See [[depends-on::bechamel]] — use the medium thickness (45g:45g:500ml). You'll need about 750ml total.

**Pasta:** 12-15 dried lasagna sheets (or fresh pasta rolled thin). If using dried, no need to pre-boil — the moisture from the sauces cooks them.

## Assembly

1. Preheat oven to 180C / 350F
2. Spread a thin layer of ragu on the bottom of a deep baking dish
3. Layer pasta sheets, ragu, bechamel, Parmesan — repeat 3-4 times
4. Finish with bechamel and Parmesan on top

## Timing

- **Bake:** 35-40 minutes at 180C, covered with foil for the first 25 minutes
- **Rest:** Let it sit 15 minutes after removing from the oven. This is critical — the layers set and slicing is clean
- **Total:** About 1 hour from assembly to plate (plus ragu time)

Lasagna reheats well. It's often better the next day once the flavors have melded.
